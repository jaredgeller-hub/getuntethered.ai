What it does

The tax record, where you already are.

Open a company's website — where you shop, pay a bill, or read the news — and Get Untethered shows you a Tax Transparency Record: the federal income tax rate that company actually paid, next to the 21% full federal rate, and the dollars involved where the filings report them.

The toolbar icon gives you the verdict at a glance. Click it for the full breakdown — the rate paid, the tax year, and what the company did with the money it kept, like stock buybacks.

Every figure comes from public records: the company's own filings with the Securities and Exchange Commission and analysis from the Institute on Taxation and Economic Policy.

The important part

It's legal. Congress wrote the rules.

These companies didn't cheat. They used deductions, credits, and provisions that Congress wrote into the tax code — accelerated depreciation, stock-option deductions, credits carried across years. All of it legal. All of it on the public record.

Get Untethered shows what companies paid, straight from their filings. It doesn't accuse anyone of breaking the law. The rules are the story — and Congress writes the rules.

That's why every record links you to a pre-written letter to your own members of Congress, built from that company's numbers. The fix isn't blaming the company for following the law. It's changing the law.

Install

One click.

Get Untethered is on the Chrome Web Store. Add it to Chrome and the verdict icon appears in your toolbar — no setup, no account.

Add to Chrome

After installing, pin it to your toolbar (click the puzzle-piece icon, then pin Get Untethered) so the verdict is always visible.

Chrome will note the extension can "read your browsing history." That permission is simply what lets it see which company's site you're on, so it can show that company's record. It never stores or transmits anything about you or your browsing — see Privacy.

Privacy

We never track you.

Get Untethered collects nothing about you. No account, no telemetry, no analytics, no error reporting. It doesn’t build a profile of you, and it never sends your browsing history anywhere.

Here is everything it does:

  • It reads the address of the site you're on, to see which company it is.
  • It looks that company up in our public tax dataset — the same companies.json file published on this site — and shows you the record.
  • It fetches that dataset from getuntethered.ai so the numbers stay current. That request carries no information about you.

The dataset is public, the extension's source is small and readable, and the filings behind every number are a matter of public record. The code is the proof.

About

Support

Common questions.

Why is almost every company red?
Partly because that’s what the filings show. But it’s also a function of which companies are here. This set was built from published analyses of corporate tax avoidance — work that starts by looking for large, profitable companies paying low rates. So the mix skews toward avoidance by construction.

Read it as a set of individual records, each true on its own. Don’t read the overall mix as representative of American business, because it isn’t a random sample.

Why does this site show “no public record”?
Most likely we don’t have that company yet. Get Untethered covers a few hundred websites belonging to large companies that file with the SEC — a small slice of the web. Private companies file nothing public, so they can’t be covered at all.

It also appears on purpose. When a company’s filings don’t give a figure we can stand behind — the numbers are missing, or they produce a rate so implausible it would be misleading — we show no record rather than print a number we can’t defend.

Where do the numbers come from?
Two public sources: companies’ own annual filings with the Securities and Exchange Commission, and analysis from the Institute on Taxation and Economic Policy. The rate shown is federal income tax on U.S. profits, against the 21% full federal rate. Data is refreshed quarterly, because companies’ fiscal years end at different times of the year.

A number looks wrong to me.
Tell us and we’ll look. Email hello@getuntethered.ai with the company and what you’re seeing. The filings behind every figure are public, so a correction is something we can check rather than argue about.

Does this work on every website?
No. It only has something to say on the websites of the companies it covers. Everywhere else the icon stays grey and the extension does nothing.

How do I remove it?
In Chrome, open the puzzle-piece menu, find Get Untethered, and choose Remove. Nothing is left behind — there’s no account and no data stored anywhere but your own browser.

Anything else: hello@getuntethered.ai

Terms

The short version.

Get Untethered is provided as-is, free of charge, for personal use. It reports figures drawn from public SEC filings and ITEP analysis. That data is a best effort, will sometimes be incomplete, and may occasionally be wrong. Where a company's numbers can't be established with confidence, the extension shows no record rather than guess. Use the information as a signal, not as a definitive judgment about any company.
